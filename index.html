Archive Overview

The Registry of Chosun Mysteries is a digital sanctuary dedicated to the exploration and preservation of mysterious entities from the Chosun era of Korean history. Our platform combines deep folkloric research with interactive storytelling, allowing visitors to engage with legends that have spanned centuries. We curate these stories through digital preservation and interactive modules.

The Nine-Tailed Mystery: Gumiho

The Gumiho is perhaps the most famous Yokai of the Chosun era. Unlike its cousins from other cultures, the Korean Gumiho is often tragic, seeking humanity through sacrifice rather than simple power. In our registry, we document the various evolutionary stages of this entity, from the Foxfire Soul to the Ascended Celestial Fox. Learn more about its history in our encyclopedia...

The Korean Dokkaebi: A Philosophical Interpretation

Unlike the monstrous 'Oni' of Japanese legend or the demonic spirits of Western folklore, the Korean Dokkaebi is a more complex, often benevolent entity born from inanimate objects. Legend says that a worn-out broom or a blood-stained tool left alone for too long could transform into a Dokkaebi. These entities are known for their love of wrestling (Ssirum) and buckwheat jelly, often using their magical clubs (Bangmangi) to grant prosperity to the honorable or teach lessons to the greedy.

Our interactive registry allows you to explore these distinctions through the 'Rebirth' simulation. By understanding the origins of these spirits, we can better appreciate the unique traditional values of the Chosun people—honoring the life within everyday objects and the balance of karma.
